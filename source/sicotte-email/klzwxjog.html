NHS Updates

3/7/2021, 8:35:40 PM

NHS Students,
This week marks the one year anniversary of COVID-19 impacting what school looks like for all of you. It's been a long, often challenging, year. But it's also been one that we have gotten through as a community because of everyone's effort to do what is needed to make all this work. We have more challenges ahead, but I am confident we will get through those as well.  

I have a handful of quick updates for you. 
  • Feedback: Please join a conversation tomorrow from 3-4 so Dr. Gutekanst, Aidan Michelow (the NHS Student Rep on the School Committee) and I can hear your thoughts on a potential full return to the building this spring or fall. Here's the link. 
  • Survey: At the end of Period C tomorrow (Blue/Green) and next Monday (Gold), NHS students will be asked to complete part two of a mental health survey. It'll mean staying in your classroom for about 10 extra minutes to make sure we have full participation. 
  • Struggling: Talk to your teachers if you are having any difficulties in classes and talk to your counselor if you are having any difficulties personally. This is a hard year for a lot of us; get the help you need to get through it. 
  • Hallways: All of our hallways are now 2 way hallways. The stairwells are all ONE way still throughout the day. At the end of the day, all stairwells can be used to exit the building. Continue to avoid gathering in groups while in the common spaces of NHS. 
  • Plumbing (yes, plumbing): We've had to shut down a number of bathrooms this year while we address plumbing issues. Many of them are caused by people flushing face masks, which are not biodegradable, and other items not fit for a toilet. Please make sure trash gets into the trash cans and not the toilets. 
  • Class Events: Your class officers and advisors are working hard to find ways to bring your classes together this spring. As we know more about what will be allowed, I encourage all of you to support your class and take part in any events or activities that can happen. 

Thanks everyone and happy almost spring!
A. Sicotte

Principal
