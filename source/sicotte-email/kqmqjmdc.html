One School, One Book Info

7/2/2021, 3:34:24 PM

NHS Students (and Families),


Happy July 4th! I hope your summer vacations are off to incredible starts! I don't really want to intrude on them, but I do want to share some information on our One School, One Book experience for the coming year.


Our plan had been to partner with Pollard Middle School and have both school communities read Trevor Noah's Born a Crime. The goal of reading his memoir about growing up in apartheid Africa was to provide us with an opportunity to learn about Noahâ€™s life in Africa and to see some of the parallels with aspects of life in America.


The intention of our OSOB is to create a shared experience to bring our community together. Unfortunately, recently Trevor Noah shared a monologue on The Daily Show that addressed the conflict between Israel and Palestine in a way that caused further division. As we looked into the monologue, we found social media posts that are not positive models for the students in our community.


All of it was concerning for those of us in both schools, but none of it is necessarily a reason to not move forward with the book itself. We appreciate and welcome difficult conversations since they can result in deep learning and growth. We were looking forward to some of the conversations his book would spark. Yet, his comments and posts would have created a new and separate layer of discussions for us as a community, one we wanted to be fully prepared for to do well. Since it all developed late in the spring, neither building felt those discussions could be well planned to support the range of perspectives that would have come up throughout the community and would have distracted from the other layers of reflections and learning we anticipated. With this recognition, we decided not to use Born a Crime as our community read for this fall.


So, for the coming year, we will not have a One School, One Book for NHS. Instead, we will engage in a One School, One Experience during the fall, which will help us achieve our goal of having a shared experience to build community throughout the school. We will also still have our annual One Day in February to continue our exploration into the identities and experiences represented throughout NHS.


You are still encouraged to read! Here is a list of book recommendations coming from our school and library staff. You'll find a great range of options for any reading interest and ability. Enjoy the great reading options here!


I also hope you enjoy the slower pace of the summer and the chance to be with family and friends. We're definitely excited to have you rejoin us in the building this fall - but we won't rush it!


See you in September!

A. Sicotte
