[Superintendent's Update] Superintendent's Update

3/18/2021, 3:44:49 PM

Dear Needham Public Schools Families:


The horrific news this week of the murders of eight people in Atlanta, six of them women of Asian descent, has shocked the nation and, locally, unsettled many Needham students, families, and staff. While the investigation is ongoing and the motivations of the individual arrested will unfold, here is what we know in the moment:


  • The victims of this brutal act are mostly women of Asian descent, and this fact has prompted many Needham staff and families who are Asian or Asian-American to worry about their students or themselves. There is a palpable level of anxiety and concern for impacted staff, for example, who stand before their students trying to calmly explain these events and reassure students even when they have trouble comforting their own children and family members.

  • Nationally, according to the Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders (AAPI), incidents of bias and hate against Asians and Asian-Americans have increased in the last year, mostly due to unfounded fears over the origin of COVID-19. Locally, there have been incidents within our own community or the Needham Schools that have impacted our Asian and Asian-American students.  These incidents were the direct result of these baseless claims and fears over the pandemic.

  • The Needham Public Schools is committed to supporting all students, including students of color who have been disproportionately impacted by acts of bias, prejudice, and hate.  As a district, we have established a framework, the Portrait of a Needham Graduate, to ensure all students develop the skills and competencies necessary to respect and appreciate the unique gifts and talents all people - regardless of race, gender, or background - bring to each other and to our school community.

  • We will continue to use our curriculum and school programs, including, for example, our elementary Racial Literacy curriculum and the secondary level’s Courageous Conversations On Race, to empower our students as they learn and grow within the Needham Public Schools.  We will offer support and counseling for all students, particularly our Asian and Asian-American students, who wish to talk about or process their experiences or concerns within the schools.  Classroom teachers, counselors, and school administrators stand ready to provide appropriate support and guidance.


These important and challenging discussions about race and racism must not be limited to our classrooms. What can parents and other adults outside of school do to help our Asian-American students, their families, and all of our students of color? The Needham Select Board, for example, through NUARI, has stepped up and is involved in an important initiative around race and race equity in our community. It seems a perfect time for all of us to engage in deeper learning about how to create a more equitable community for all.


For additional information and resources about what we are doing in the schools to support this work, please check out the district’s Equity website and our Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Resources website.


Respectfully,



Dan Gutekanst
