[Superintendent's Update] Vaccine Clinics for Needham Youths

5/11/2021, 2:35:54 PM

Dear Needham Public Schools Families:

I am pleased to announce that in partnership with Waltham-based pharmacy, PelMeds, the Needham Public Schools, in consultation with the Town of Needham Health Department, will host two COVID-19 vaccine clinics for Needham's young people enrolled in public or private school, one on Saturday, May 15th at Pollard and a second clinic at Needham High School on Saturday, May 22nd. Follow up clinics for the second dose will also be scheduled at both locations.  Many thanks to PelMeds President and Needham parent, Bhuren Patel for offering this opportunity!

As you may know, yesterday, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) announced the emergency use of the Pfizer vaccine in adolescents aged 12 and up, and tomorrow the Centers for Disease Control (CDC) will meet to discuss and vote on additional guidance for the use of the Pfizer vaccine in young people. In a press release, American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) President Lee Savio Beers, MD, "applauded the FDA’s action, calling it a “critically important step in bringing life-saving vaccines to children and adolescents. Our youngest generations have shouldered heavy burdens over the past year, and the vaccine is a hopeful sign that they will be able to begin to experience all the activities that are so important for their health and development."  More information, including vaccine information and FAQs about the vaccine can be found here: American Academy of Pediatrics.

Pending the CDC's final guidance expected tomorrow, Pelmeds has availability for any Needham youth and Boston resident students participating in the Needham METCO Program who are age 12 and up this Saturday, May 15th in the Pollard gyms from 9:00 a.m. until 6:00 p.m.  Please sign up here:  Register child age 12 and up for COVID vaccine clinic.  Consent forms and other information are available when you register.  Registration for the second clinic at Needham High School will be made available soon.

I encourage families to register now for this important vaccine clinic.  Please contact your child's pediatrician should you have any questions.
