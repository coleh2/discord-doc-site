Supporting our Asian American Community

3/17/2021, 9:04:29 PM

NHS Students,
I am grappling with the recent murders in Atlanta right now, and how they may fit into the ongoing series of attacks on Asian Americans and Asian American owned businesses and properties over the last year. There has been a tremendous surge in anti-Asian American comments and actions, particularly over the year of the pandemic, leaving many people across the country unsafe and targeted. 

More locally, these actions over the recent months have left students, staff, and NHS community members concerned for their safety and wondering about their community around them. 

In the Needham schools, we say Black lives matter clearly and resoundingly because for over 400 years that has not been consistently demonstrated in America. We must be equally clear in our support of Asian Americans in our community and throughout the country, especially right now to counterbalance the recent offenses and attacks. 

I implore each of you to do your part - both big and small - to make sure that all members of our community are, and feel, welcomed and a deep sense of safety and belonging. No one should be without that. 

And I want to emphasize to all of you that while in the NHS community, we will take care of each and every member. NHS will remain a safe and welcoming place for every student and staff member. Any acts of hate or offense will be addressed quickly and firmly. Please let your counselor, assistant principal, or me know if there is any way that we can support you now or in the future. 

In unity,
A. Sicotte
